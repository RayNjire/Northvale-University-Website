WHY US

The University is organised into eleven separate academic units, ten faculties and the Radcliffe Institute for advanced study with campuses throughout the Boston metropolitan area. It's a 209 acre (85ha) main campus on Northvale Yard in Massachusetts. The Business schools and athletic facilities, including the stadium that was invested in by the International Technological Sports Union, are located across the Charles River in the Allston neighbourhood of Boston and the medical, dental and public health schools are in the longwood medical area.

Northvale has one of the largest financial endowment of any educational institution in the world, standing at $30.1 billion as of August 2016.

And if that’s not satisfying enough, here are five more reasons why you need to study at Northvale University:

  1. We are ranked in the top 5% worldwide.

    2. By choosing to study with us, you’ll benefit from degrees that are informed by our world-class research, expert knowledge and industry connection.

  2. We are America’s fastest growing university precinct.

    3. With more than $320 million worth of upgrades to our campuses all over the state. With these being rolled out for this generation and the many others to come, you’ll learn in an exciting and progressive environment.

  3. We’re leaders in student mobility.

    4. The Northvale’s study abroad program gives you an opportunity to complete one or two semesters of study in America and earn credits towards your home degree while experiencing an amazing culture and original lifestyle.

  4. We offer a practical innovative approach to teaching.

    5. Northvale offers work integrated internships that allow you to undertake a work related placement as part of your studies and prepares you to be competitive in the job market with our Industry Mentoring Program.

  5. We help you reach your potential.

    6. As an international student at Northvale, you’ll have access to dedicated, International Student Advisors and technology to help you reach your potential while studying and living in America.
