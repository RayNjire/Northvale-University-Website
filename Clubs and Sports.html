CLUBS AND SPORTS

As Northvale University's student body grows not only will you be able to join existing clubs, you'll also play an important role in creating new ones. Whether you join the debate team or adventure club, audition to sing Acapella, learn Arabic, or a couple of other foreign languages like mandarin and Russian, or join the film society for future actors and directors, or maybe you’re more of a talkative fellow, well then, joining the NUT radio station is the solution for you, you could also join the fashion society or edit submissions for the arts and humanities journal Electra Street. You're sure to find an activity that suits you and with a growing student body and an infinite range of interests, you can start just about any other club you can imagine. If you can dream it up, we’ll help you get your ideas off the ground.

Sports

Students have many opportunities to compete and stay in shape through athletic teams, club sports, intramurals, physical and educational classes, and individual workouts. Our intercollegiate teams compete in national and regional sports tournaments; from winning the top prize in in the university category in the Dubai International Dragon Boat Festival, to competing against other students in the region in a football and soccer tournament. Teams have travelled to Jordan to run in the annual "Dead 2 Red" Relay race from the Dead Sea to the Red Sea.

Other students take part in Northvale’s wide range of club sports and intramurals with games such as flag soccer, inner-tube water polo, volleyball, tennis, Hockey (With its tournaments going on all winter), squash, badminton, swimming, cricket, ten-pin bowling, acrobatics, a variety of snow sports, pool, golf, netball, volleyball, rugby, dance, basketball and many more.

The Athletic Department Staff ensures students get the most out of the sea, sun, sand and fields that the Northvale University has to offer, these include the fields and facilities of the places we have made partners with throughout the city. This helps the students find balance, stress relief, class cohesion, fun and leadership opportunities outside the classroom.
